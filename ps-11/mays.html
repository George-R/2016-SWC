Willie Mays joined the MLB in 1950, signed by the New York Giants and an amateur free agent. In 1953 Mays missed the entire baseball season, drafted by the US Army durnig the Korean War. He returned to the Giants in 1954, leading the team to a World Series Title.

It was "The Catch" in the eight inning of the 1954 World Series that would become the defining image of Mays. In 1956 he became the first player in the National League to join the 30-30 club, hitting 36 home runs and stealing 40 bases.
