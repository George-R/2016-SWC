Barry Bonds entered the MLB in 1986, drafted 6th overall by the Pittsburgh Pirates. He spent his first seven years with the Pirates and in 1993 signed for a record (at the time) of $43.75 million over six years with the San Francisco Giants. On 7 August 2007, Bonds hit his 756th home-run, passing Hank Aaron on the all-time list.

Since 2003, Barry Bonds has been accused of steroid use during his pro-playing career and was inicted on perjury and obstruction of justive on November 15, 2007.
