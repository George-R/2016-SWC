Hank Aaron entered the MLB in 1952, signed as a free agent by the Boston Braves. The team moved to Milwaukee in 1953, Aaron making his MLB debut in 1954. On 8 April 1974, Aaron hit home run number 715, passing Babe Ruth for the number one spot on the all-time Home Runs list. This milestone received a mixed reaction in a world full of racial tensions.

Hank Aaron holds the record for most All-Star appearances with 21 during his 23-year-old career.
